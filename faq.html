Answers

I'm not a BSAC diver/member. Can I still attend?

Of course! We welcome all divers to attend and refresh their knowledge, or even just see how we do things at RUBSAC. If you're thinking of crossing over from another agency, there's a handy chart here.

I'm not currently paid up with BSAC or with my club. Can I still attend?

Of course! We welcome all divers to attend, further or refresh their knowledge. However, depending on your exact circumstances you may not be able to get credit for attendance as part of the course if you aren’t a paid up member with BSAC and/or your local club. Get in touch with your local club’s Diving Officer or Training Officer for more clarity on their policy.

Back to top


I'm not currently enrolled on the course and/or I have not completed the prior qualification. Can I still attend?

Absolutely! We welcome all divers to attend and further their knowledge. However, depending on your exact circumstances you may not be able to get credit for attendance as part of the course. BSAC encourages progressive learning, and each lecture builds on knowledge you will have picked up in prior lectures/qualifications. You may find concepts difficult to understand or downright confusing if you have skipped something. Sometimes you will not be permitted to get signed off for a lecture until you have completed a pre-requisite. Get in touch with your local club’s Diving Officer or Training Officer for more clarity on what lectures you ought to be taking part in, based on your current level of study.

How do I get signed off in my logbook for the sessions I've attended?

At the end of each lecture we give out a link to a form. This needs to be filled out straight away – it is locked out a couple of hours after the lecture completes.

You will be asked to fill out your name, email address and your club. Once you have submitted this information, the next page gives you an instructor number to enter in your log book along with a code which you need to put into the signature panel for that lecture. We have confirmed with BSAC HQ that its ok to do this, and their answer is that in the short term this is fine. They are looking into more secure methods of doing this and hope to have a system in place very soon.

Can we get a recording of the lecture please?

Sorry no – there are a few good reasons why;

Firstly, BSAC does not currently support teaching using videos as an alternative to the interactive lectures (unlike say, PADI or SSI, who rely on a combination of videos and seminars). This is not to say PADI or SSI are wrong. Merely that here at BSAC, we place a lot of value on the interactive nature of our teaching sessions. If you don’t have that experience, some of that value is lost.

Secondly, there are data protection issues with us distributing a recording which includes attendees’ interactions without their express permission.

Thirdly, the presenter does not necessarily consent to their lecture being recorded.

Sorry about that!

I forgot to fill out the form, can I still get credit for the lecture in my logbook?

Probably not – we are at pains to explain about the form in the lecture, and it is our only real way of ascertaining if someone took part. There are other ways, but they are very laborious and we simply don’t have time to go digging through dozens of pages of computer records to find out who was and wasn’t here.

Sorry about that!
